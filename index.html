AI-Integrated Educational Examples

Selected interactive educational examples developed by the Financial Intelligence for Consumer Well-being Lab. These modules demonstrate how interactive and AI-integrated learning materials can support financial services education, ethics training, and applied client-case analysis.

Chapter 1
Introduction to Ethics
Foundational definitions, frameworks, and the language of ethical practice in financial services.
Chapter 2
Ethical Theories
Utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics, fiduciary principles, and ethical reasoning.
Chapter 3
Ethical Decision-Making
Structured decision-making frameworks and applied professional judgment.
Chapter 4
The Ethical Mind
Behavioral and psychological foundations of ethical and unethical decision-making.
Chapter 5
Regulatory Frameworks
Historical and institutional foundations of financial regulation.
Chapter 6
From Misconduct to Oversight
Regulatory enforcement, professional oversight, and misconduct prevention.
Chapter 7
Risk Mitigation
Controls, audits, governance, training, and compliance systems.
Chapter 8
Client-Facing Ethics
Integrity, fairness, accountability, and transparency in advisor-client relationships.
Chapter 9
Insider Trading and Market Manipulation
Ethical and regulatory issues involving market integrity.
Chapter 10
Data Privacy and Cybersecurity
Privacy, cybersecurity, data governance, and client protection.
Chapter 11
Fair Treatment of Clients
Procedural, interactional, and distributive fairness in financial services.
Chapter 12
Firm-Level Ethics and Culture
Organizational culture, incentives, governance, and leadership.
Chapter 13
Developing an Ethical Firm Culture
Practical approaches to building and sustaining ethical firm culture.
Chapter 14
Emerging Trends
AI, RegTech, distributed ledger technology, open banking, and cloud systems.
Chapter 15
Ethics in a Digital Age
Technology governance, AI oversight, and digital-age compliance challenges.
Chapter 16
Conclusion and Future Directions
Integrating ethics, compliance, professional responsibility, and future practice.

These sample modules are working examples and may be updated over time.
